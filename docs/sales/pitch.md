## The 2-minute pitch

*Use this in a first call, a warm intro meeting, or when someone wants a bit more after the 30-second version.*

---

**The problem**

Right now, a typical graduate program runs on four or five separate tools. There's an ATS for the pipeline. A separate psychometric platform. A video interview tool. Spreadsheets for scheduling. And something else entirely for onboarding and development. None of them talk to each other properly.

The result? Program managers spend an entire day just to generate an initial shortlist. Manual screening, manual data transfer, manual everything. And the insight they built about candidates during assessment — the scores, the dimensions, what each person is actually good at — that all disappears the moment the offer goes out. The development team never sees it.

The industry has focused on Attract and Select for decades. Everything after the offer letter is an afterthought.

**What Talent Edge does**

We cover the full lifecycle — eight stages, from Attract through to Track Talent — in one product. No integration tax. No data disappearing between systems.

The assessment is AI-powered, measures five validated dimensions of potential, and the scoring carries through into development planning after hire. The platform is self-service, so program managers can configure questions, branding, and scoring weights themselves — no developer involvement, no change request to a vendor.

Candidate engagement is automated from assessment through to onboarding. Candidates get what they need. Program managers stop fielding the same questions 200 times.

**Why it's different**

Against Amberjack: they're bespoke and managed-service only. Every change costs money and takes time. We're self-service, full lifecycle, and built with equity at the centre.

Against Grad-Engage: they do one thing — keep candidates warm between offer and start date. We do that too, as part of a connected platform that handles everything else as well.

Against building it yourself with five tools: one platform, one place, one source of truth. The assessment data informs the development plan. That connection is the whole point.

## The 10-minute pitch

### Open: the thing the industry doesn't say out loud

There's something the graduate recruitment industry rarely names out loud.

Traditional assessment processes systematically favour candidates who've already been advantaged. The ones who perform well in behavioural interviews are usually the ones who've had a better education, completed prestigious internships, travelled, volunteered, and didn't have to work a second job through uni.

The student who graduated with a 3.5 GPA while working 30 hours a week often loses to the student with a 3.8 and a gap year in Europe. Not because they have less potential. Because they've had fewer opportunities to demonstrate it in the ways traditional assessment rewards.

We built Talent Edge around a different question. Not "what have you done?" but "what are you capable of?"

That's the philosophy behind everything we've built. And it's what makes the product different in ways that actually matter.

### Stage 1 — Attract

Right now, your employer brand probably lives in one system. Your application form is in another. Candidates land on something that doesn't feel cohesive, and your program team has no visibility into which track or role a candidate is actually interested in before they even start the process.

In Talent Edge, the attraction stage is part of the platform. Candidates land on a branded program page, choose their career track — Finance, Technology, People and Culture, whatever your tracks are — and that choice travels with them through every stage that follows. The assessment adapts to their track. The development plan knows what role they're heading into.

---

### Stage 2 — Assess

Your assessment is probably run by a third-party platform. And every time you want to change anything — the wording of a question, the competency labels in a report, the way the scoring is weighted — you raise a ticket and wait.

If you're with Amberjack, the cheap option is a logo slapped on a generic test. The expensive option is a fully custom build. There's nothing in between.

We built self-service configuration into the core of the platform. Program managers edit questions, adjust branding, change scoring weights themselves, without involving us. No tickets. No waiting. No invoices for a change that took ten minutes.

The assessment itself measures five dimensions: Adaptability, Cognitive Agility, Emotional Intelligence, Collaboration, and Drive. Scenario-based questions, Likert scales, forced choice, cognitive tasks. Validated methodology. And because it's measuring potential, not performance history, it levels the playing field for candidates who haven't had the same starting point.

### Stage 3 — Select

Your program manager spends a full day on initial screening. They're in a spreadsheet, cross-referencing scores, flagging accessibility needs, trying to remember which candidate was which. It takes a long time and it's not a good use of anyone's time.

In Talent Edge, the pipeline is a kanban board. Every candidate, every stage, all in one view. Filters by score band. Search by name, university, degree. AI-generated screening summaries on every profile — a structured recommendation based on dimension scores, written for the recruiter, not the candidate.

You can compare two or three candidates side by side. You can bulk shortlist. You can see which dimensions are strongest and weakest across the whole cohort before you start making decisions.

And candidates who declared accessibility needs at registration? They're flagged automatically on their profile. No email chase. No manual list.

---

### Stage 4 — Offer and Keep Warm

You make the offers. Then nothing. The candidate accepts, gets a confirmation email, and then doesn't hear from you for three months. That's where you lose them. They accept another offer. They go cold. They show up on day one with half their enthusiasm already gone.

There's a whole product in the market — Grad-Engage — that exists solely to solve this problem. It gets traction because nothing else does it properly.

Keep Warm in Talent Edge is a first-class lifecycle stage. Automated touchpoints between offer acceptance and start date. A welcome video. A countdown to their start date. An onboarding checklist. All automated, all tracked, all visible to the program manager in one place.

You don't need a separate tool for this. It's part of the platform.

---

### Stage 5 — Develop

This is the part that doesn't exist anywhere else.

The insight you built about a candidate during assessment — their scores across five dimensions, what they're strong at, where they need support — should inform how you develop them once they're hired. But in every other platform on the market, that data disappears the moment the offer is accepted. Recruitment and development are two different systems, two different teams, two different conversations.

In Talent Edge, those scores travel with the candidate into their development plan. A candidate who scored high on Cognitive Agility but low on Collaboration gets a different set of development goals than one who's the inverse. That signal exists. We just don't throw it away.

Development goals, progress tracking, manager visibility — it's all there. And it connects back to the assessment that started the whole journey.
