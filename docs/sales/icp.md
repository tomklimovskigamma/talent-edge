# Ideal Customer Profile

A practical targeting guide. Use this to decide quickly whether a prospect is worth pursuing now, worth parking, or not for us.

---

## Who we sell to

**Mid-to-large Australian employers with structured annual graduate intake programs.**

The sweet spot is organisations hiring 10–100 graduates per year through a defined intake cycle — application window, assessment, interview, offer, onboard. Not ad-hoc hiring. A structured program.

They're almost always already running:

- An ATS — PageUp, Workday, Success Factors, Springboard, or similar
- A separate psychometric assessment platform — Amberjack is the most common, but not the only one

The pain we solve is real and recurring for these organisations. They're not looking to be educated on why grad recruitment matters. They're frustrated by one or more of:

- **Assessment inflexibility** — changing a question or competency label costs money and takes weeks
- **Tool fragmentation** — five platforms that don't talk to each other, and a program manager doing the integration by hand
- **Manual screening overhead** — initial shortlisting takes a full day or more of spreadsheet work
- **Disconnect between recruitment and development** — potential data collected during selection disappears the moment the grad starts

**Sectors to prioritise:**

- Financial services (banks, insurance, superannuation)
- Professional services — Big 4 accounting, law firms with structured grad programs
- Government — state and federal agencies with annual cohort intakes
- Resources and mining — structured technical graduate programs
- Large retailers with formal grad programs (not stores hiring casually)

## Who's in the room

You will typically encounter four people. Know what each one needs.

| Persona | Typical title | What they care about | What resonates |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Program Manager** | Graduate Program Manager / Early Careers Lead / Graduate Recruitment Coordinator | Day-to-day pain: screening time, scheduling chaos, manual handoffs, candidate experience complaints | "Bulk shortlisting replaces a full day of Excel screening." "Candidates self-book, reschedule, and cancel without calling you." "One system instead of five." Show them the pipeline board. This is your champion. |
| **The HR Director** | Head of Talent / Director of Early Careers / HR Director | Equity and DEI reporting, program ROI, graduate retention rates, metrics they can take to the board | "Assess for potential, not privilege." Lifecycle tracking. Cohort insights. They're buying the philosophy as much as the product. |
| **The Procurement Buyer** | Head of Procurement / IT / Vendor Management | Cost vs. the incumbent, integration risk, data security, contract terms | We sit on top of their ATS — we don't replace it, so integration risk is low. Self-service configuration means no bespoke dev costs. Bring line-item comparisons. |
| **The Grad** *(influencer, not buyer)* | Recent graduate, program alumni, campus ambassador | Was the process fair? Accessible? Did they get feedback? | AI chat during assessment, accommodation requests handled without phone calls, transparent scoring. They don't sign contracts, but their experience shapes the Program Manager's view of the platform. |

## Who we don't pursue

Being clear about this saves time on both sides.

**Fewer than 10 grad hires per year.** The economics don't work for them or for us. Be polite. Don't ghost them — some will grow. Park the contact and revisit in 12–18 months.

**Mid-contract with Amberjack on a multi-year deal, no trigger event.** If they're locked in and there's no obvious pain, don't push. Note the renewal date. Follow up 6 months before it. A trigger event (new HR director, program restructure, budget review) changes this calculation — keep an ear out.

**No structured graduate program.** If they're hiring graduates on an ad-hoc basis with no intake cycle, they need to build a program before they need a platform. That's an education sale, not a product sale. Not our job right now.

**Want a fully outsourced managed service.** Some organisations don't want a platform — they want someone else to run the whole recruitment function. That's Amberjack's core strength. We're not competing there. Acknowledge it and move on.
