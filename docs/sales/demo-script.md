# Demo Script

This is what you SAY while you click. The Demo Guide (docs/demo-guide.md) tells you where to click. This doc tells you what story to tell while you're clicking.

**Setup:** Go to [talent-edge-ten.vercel.app](https://talent-edge-ten.vercel.app). You'll see two cards. You're starting with **Admin** (Sarah Chen, Graduate Program Manager). Run Path 1 first — it's the main demo. Path 2 (Graduate) is a bonus if time allows or they ask what candidates see.

---

## Path 1 — Admin (primary demo path)

Start on the home screen. Click the **Admin** card.

---

### Dashboard

**You're showing:** The command centre — live metrics, pipeline funnel, top candidates, cohort intelligence, and the full eight-stage lifecycle view.

**You say:** "This is where Sarah, the program manager, starts her day. She's running the Meridian Group 2026 graduate intake — Finance, Technology, and People and Culture tracks. One screen tells her where everything stands: 187 applicants in the program, 142 assessed, 43 shortlisted, 9 offers out.

Scroll up to the banner across the top — those are the eight stages of the graduate lifecycle, from Attract through to Track Talent. Most tools show you four. Attract, screen, select, offer. We show you all eight, because the program doesn't end when the offer goes out.

Now look at the Cohort Intelligence card. This is telling Sarah that Cognitive Agility is the strongest dimension across her cohort, Collaboration is the weakest, and Finance track is outscoring Technology. That's computed directly from live assessment data — it's not a report someone built in Excel over a weekend."

**The point:** Single pane of glass, full lifecycle visibility, and data-driven cohort insights that help Sarah make decisions before she opens a single candidate profile.

**If they ask:**
- "Are those numbers real?" — "Demo data, but the metrics are computed from candidate records the same way they would be in production. Change a candidate's stage and the numbers update."
- "What are the development stages?" — "Onboard, Develop, Track Talent — those are live on Hired candidates' profiles today. Automated development plans from assessment scores are next on the roadmap."

---

### Pipeline (Kanban)

**You're showing:** All 187 candidates arranged by stage — Applied, Assessed, Shortlisted, Interview, Offer, Hired — with search, filters, and bulk actions.

**You say:** "Here's the pipeline. Sarah has 187 applicants and needs to get to 20 hires. The first thing she does is filter down. Click 'High Potential' — that's anyone scoring 80 or above. Down to the candidates worth a closer look.

Now search for 'Melbourne' inside that filter — she can compose them. Only high-potential candidates from Melbourne universities. This replaces a morning in a spreadsheet.

Once she's decided who to move forward — she ticks the checkboxes on the Assessed cards she wants, and clicks 'Shortlist selected'. All of them move at once. What used to take a full day of manual cross-referencing is a two-minute action."

**The point:** Screening efficiency with a quantifiable time saving. The bulk shortlisting flow is the moment prospects start doing mental arithmetic on what this would save their team.

**If they ask:**
- "How are the scores calculated?" — "Five-dimension psychometric assessment — I'll show you the full breakdown on a candidate profile in a moment."
- "Can we filter by track as well?" — "Track filtering is on the roadmap. Right now you can filter by score band and search by name, university, or degree."

---

### Accessibility Flag (Pipeline)

**You're showing:** The violet badge on a candidate card in the pipeline board.

**You say:** "Before we open a profile — see this small violet badge on Sophie Williams' card? She declared accessibility needs during registration. Extended time required, dyslexia. That flag is right there in the pipeline — Sarah doesn't have to dig through a registration form or chase an email. It follows the candidate through every screen she'll ever see them on."

**The point:** Accessibility-first design that respects both candidate dignity and recruiter compliance. The information surfaces automatically — no manual process.

**If they ask:**
- "What accommodations do you support?" — "The candidate describes their needs in free text during registration. The system surfaces it — your team decides what adjustment to make. It's designed to be flexible rather than prescriptive."
- "Is that visible to the candidate?" — "No — it's an admin-only flag."

---

### Candidate Profile

**You're showing:** The full candidate profile — header with score ring, AI screening summary, potential radar, assessment timeline, and report generation.

**You say:** "Click any candidate to open their profile. This is everything Sarah needs to make a decision about one person.

At the top — name, university, degree, their overall potential score, and when they applied. If they have accessibility needs, there's a badge here too.

Below that is the AI Screening Summary. This is written specifically for the recruiter — it takes the dimension scores and turns them into a plain-English recommendation. Something like: 'Strong analytical profile with high Cognitive Agility. Collaboration is a relative gap but above average. Recommend for shortlist.' Sarah doesn't have to interpret a radar chart — the system does the interpretation and she decides whether she agrees.

The potential radar is below that, if she wants to go deeper. And in the top-right — Generate Report. One click. That produces the structured feedback report your candidates are expecting after every assessment: scores, interpretations, strengths, development areas, next steps. Done."

**The point:** AI-assisted screening that speeds up decision-making without removing the recruiter's judgment. Automated report generation closes a loop that most programs handle manually or not at all.

**If they ask:**
- "Do candidates see their scores?" — "They see the shape of the radar — the profile across the five dimensions — but not the numbers. Scores are for recruiters. We think that's the right balance."
- "Can we customise the report?" — "The report template is standard today. Customisation is on the roadmap."
- "What does the AI summary actually do?" — "It's keyed to score bands — different language depending on where someone sits. It's not making things up — it's translating numbers into plain English."

---

### Candidate Comparison

**You're showing:** Two candidates side-by-side in the comparison drawer — grouped bar chart and AI summaries.

**You say:** "Sarah's got three candidates shortlisted for the final Finance spot. She wants to compare two of them directly. She ticks the checkboxes on their cards — watch the drawer slide up at the bottom of the screen.

Now she clicks Compare. Side by side: their dimension scores on a grouped bar chart, their AI summaries underneath. Candidate A is stronger on Cognitive Agility. Candidate B scores higher on Collaboration. That's a concrete, data-based conversation — not 'I think I preferred the first one I spoke to.'"

**The point:** Data-driven hiring decisions, not gut feel. The comparison view makes the tradeoffs between candidates visible and discussable.

**If they ask:**
- "Can we compare more than 2?" — "Up to 3 at a time."
- "Can we export the comparison?" — "Not yet — that's on the roadmap."

---

### Interview Scheduling

**You're showing:** The scheduling modal — five time slots, select and confirm flow.

**You say:** "One more thing in the pipeline before we go to settings. Hover over any candidate in the Interview column — you'll see a 'Schedule interview' button. Click it. Sarah sees five available slots across three days. She selects one, clicks 'Send Calendar Invite' — done. Candidate is booked. No email back and forth."

**The point:** Scheduling as part of the workflow — not a separate calendar tool or an email chain.

**If they ask:**
- "Does this connect to our actual calendar?" — "This is the demo flow. Real calendar integration — Google, Outlook — is on the roadmap."

---

### Assessment Config

**You're showing:** Settings → Assessment Config — question language, client branding, scoring weights.

**You say:** "Now here's the one I most enjoy showing people. Navigate to Settings, then Assessment Config.

This is the page that would cost you a bespoke engagement with Amberjack. Inside our managed engagement, your team controls this config without ever raising a change request. You want to call it 'Strategic Thinking' instead of 'Cognitive Agility' — you do it here. Different wording for Finance versus Technology track — done here. Logo, brand colour, header text — here. Scoring weights per track — here.

No change request. No waiting two weeks. No invoice for a change that took ten minutes. Your program manager does it herself on a Tuesday afternoon — your team stays in the driver's seat inside the engagement, without the vendor bottleneck."

**The point:** Config control inside a managed engagement is the clearest differentiator against Amberjack. With Amberjack, every change goes through a bespoke request and a bill. With us, the same managed service gives your team direct control. Don't rush past this screen.

**If they ask:**
- "Can we use our own competency framework entirely?" — "Yes — you type your labels right here. The underlying psychometric model stays the same, but everything the candidate and recruiter sees uses your language."
- "What about changing the actual questions?" — "Question wording is editable. Adding entirely new questions is a deeper change — we'd work with you on that."

---

### Closing the loop: video and assessment centres

**This is not a separate slide — weave this into the demo naturally before you close.** It's how Dave stays ahead of questions that would otherwise land as gaps.

**On video interviews:**

"Our platform covers application, psychometric testing, and video interview capture. Video is on our next release — for now, we integrate with existing video tools if you use Modern Hire or HireVue, and our services team handles the initial screening conversations. So the workflow is already there — it just moves through the integration point until the native capability ships."

**On Assessment Centres:**

"ACs are where human judgment matters most. Our people design and run the AC face-to-face — that's part of what you get in the managed engagement. The platform captures the scores from AC assessors back into the candidate profile — so your psych data, AC ratings, and interview feedback all live in one place. That's how you end up with a complete picture for the offer decision. One profile, full picture, no spreadsheet in the middle."

**The point:** Be the one who names the gaps before they ask. Prospects trust a partner who's honest about where the roadmap sits over one who gets caught out.

**If they ask:**
- "When does native video land?" — "It's the headline item on our next release. I don't want to give you a date I can't stand behind, but it's not far out."
- "So the AC data actually comes back into the system?" — "Yes — AC assessor scores feed directly into the candidate profile alongside the psych data. So when you're making the offer decision, you're looking at one complete record."

## Path 2 — Graduate (bonus path)

Run this if time allows, or if someone asks "what do candidates actually see?" Takes about 4 minutes. Go back to the home screen and choose **Graduate** (Jordan Lee).

---

### Registration

**You're showing:** The candidate registration form and accessibility accommodations card.

**You say:** "Here's what your candidates see. Jordan's details are pre-filled — in a real program this is where they enter name, university, degree. They pick their career track: Finance, Technology, or People and Culture.

And then this second card: accessibility accommodations. They tick a checkbox, a text area appears, they describe what they need. It's self-service, it's private, and it flows directly to the recruiter view we just saw. No phone call. No email to an inbox nobody monitors. They declare it here, it appears on their pipeline card."

**The point:** Accessibility-first by design, not as an afterthought.

---

### Assessment

**You're showing:** Five sections, 20 questions, multiple question types.

**You say:** "This is the assessment itself. Five sections, 20 questions total. Each section measures one dimension of potential: Adaptability, Cognitive Agility, Emotional Intelligence, Collaboration, Drive.

The question types vary — you'll see situational judgement scenarios, Likert scales, forced choice questions, a sequence puzzle for Cognitive Agility, emotion recognition from facial cues for EI. Jordan's in the Finance track, so the scenarios are about financial services contexts. A Technology track candidate gets the same structure but different scenario wording.

The philosophy here is important: every question is measuring how someone thinks and how they respond to challenges — not how polished their CV is or how many internships they've done. The student who worked 30 hours a week through uni gets a fair go."

**The point:** Rigorous but accessible assessment that measures potential, not privilege. [ENRICH: Paula item #3 — deeper assessment type knowledge lets Dave explain the psychometric model with more authority]

**If they ask:**
- "How long does it take?" — "About 25–30 minutes. Candidates are told upfront."

---

### AI Chat Assistant

**You're showing:** The floating chat button and pre-answered FAQ panel.

**You say:** "At any point during the assessment, Jordan can click this button in the bottom corner. A panel opens with five pre-answered questions — how long is this, what's being measured, when do I get feedback, what about accessibility, who sees my data. They get an instant answer instead of sending an email to the program mailbox that someone has to answer 200 times a week.

This is a small thing but it meaningfully reduces the volume of candidate queries your team handles during an intake."

**The point:** Candidate experience that respects candidates' time and reduces admin load for the program team.

---

### Results

**You're showing:** The radar chart of Jordan's potential profile.

**You say:** "Once Jordan completes all five sections, they see their results. The radar shows their profile shape — high on Cognitive Agility, working on Collaboration. Clean, respectful, useful. They see the shape but not the numbers — those live in the recruiter view we saw earlier. We think that's the right balance: candidates get meaningful feedback, recruiters keep the detail they need to make decisions."

**The point:** Fair, transparent candidate feedback without giving away the scoring mechanics.

After Path 1 (and Path 2 if you ran it), close with this:

"What you've seen is the platform. But Talent Edge isn't a software sale — it's a managed engagement. Our services team runs the face-to-face work: Assessment Centre design and delivery, interview facilitation, and early careers advisory. The platform is what makes the managed service better than anything else in the market — modern infrastructure, live data, AI screening, full lifecycle coverage. You get the partner and the system."

Then move to what's coming next:

"Here's what's on the roadmap. The headline item: video interview capability built natively into the platform. Right now we integrate with Modern Hire and HireVue — the next release brings it home. And alongside that:

1. Score percentile display — "Top X% of cohort" on every pipeline card and profile, so Sarah can see at a glance how each candidate ranks against the cohort.
2. Interview scorecard — structured rating form for candidates at interview stage. Communication, Cultural Fit, Problem Solving — consistent scoring across every interviewer.
3. Bulk reject with email preview — select the candidates you're not progressing, edit the rejection email, send them all at once. One action instead of 200 individual emails.
4. AI-generated development plans — when a candidate is hired, the system auto-generates their development goals from their assessment results. Weakest dimension gets a targeted activity plan. The data from screening finally informs what happens after hire."

"The assessment insight you built during selection doesn't disappear when someone signs the offer. That's the thing no one else has built. And that's where we're going."

## Common objections during the demo

See `docs/sales/objections.md` for full handling. Quick reference for what comes up mid-demo:

- **"We already use Amberjack"** — "Amberjack is a strong managed service — same delivery model as us. The question is which platform you want underneath the engagement. Ours covers more of the lifecycle, assessment config moves without a change request, and the screening intelligence is built in."
- **"We just renewed our ATS"** — "We're not replacing your ATS — we integrate with it. PageUp and Workday are already shown as connected on the dashboard."
- **"Psychometrics don't predict performance"** — "You're right that traditional behavioural assessments often don't. We're not measuring performance history — we're measuring potential. Different question, different answer."
- **"This looks early-stage"** — "You're right that we're early. What you're seeing is a working product, not a slide deck. Early partners shape what we build next."
- **"Do you offer Assessment Centres?"** — "Yes, we run them face-to-face. Assessment Centre design and delivery is part of the managed engagement — our people run the day. The platform captures the assessor scores back into the candidate profile so the data lives alongside the psych results and interview feedback."
- **"Do you do video interviews?"** — "Platform captures the video, our people review it. Native video recording in the platform ships on our next release — for today's integrations we use Modern Hire or HireVue. The workflow is already there."
