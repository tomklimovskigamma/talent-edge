# Battle Card: Amberjack

**Audience:** Dave (sales). Use this when Amberjack is the incumbent or named competitor.

---

## Positioning

Amberjack is a managed assessment service. Talent Edge is a self-service recruitment and development platform. They run your assessment for you. We give you the tools to run it yourself — and then connect it to everything that happens after.

---

## Side by side

| Dimension | Amberjack | Talent Edge |

| What they are | Managed assessment service + outsourced recruitment | Self-service assessment and development platform |

## When we win

- **Program managers frustrated by inflexibility.** Changing assessment wording, swapping competency labels, adjusting a report format — everything in Amberjack requires a change request and a bill. Teams that want to iterate quickly hit a wall.
- **Companies caring about equity and DEI.** "Assess for potential, not privilege" is a more honest and more defensible story. Traditional behavioural assessments favour candidates who've had access to internships, top-tier education, and overseas experience. A potential-based model levels the playing field.
- **Keep Warm gap.** Candidate dropout between offer acceptance and start date is a real and costly problem. Amberjack doesn't cover this phase — organisations either leave it unmanaged or bolt on Grad-Engage separately. Talent Edge includes Keep Warm as a native, connected lifecycle phase.
- **Want assessment data to inform development.** Amberjack stops at selection. The potential data gathered during screening never makes it into a development plan. Talent Edge connects assessment through to Track Talent — the data doesn't disappear after hire.
- **Value self-service and speed over white-glove.** Teams that want to own their configuration, move quickly between intakes, and not wait on a vendor queue are a natural fit. If the answer to "can I change that myself?" is always "no", that's a pain point we solve.
- **Amberjack contract up for renewal.** Renewal is the trigger event. Mid-contract deals are rare — target accounts approaching end of term.

## When we lose

- **Want fully outsourced managed service.** If the client wants a vendor to handle everything — assessment design, candidate management, scheduling, reporting — Amberjack's managed model is genuinely strong. We don't try to compete here.
- **Mid-contract, no trigger event.** Without a renewal window or a live pain point, there's no lever. Qualify out early and stay warm.
- **Value established brand and enterprise credentials over capability.** Amberjack has strong market recognition in Australian graduate recruitment. For procurement-driven decisions where brand credibility outweighs product fit, that works against us.
- **Need Assessment Centre design and delivery.** Group exercises, role plays, assessor training, structured debrief facilitation — Amberjack offers this as part of their managed service. We don't cover Assessment Centre delivery yet.

---

## Landmines

Questions Dave plants early to make Amberjack limitations visible. Ask these in discovery — they surface pain without naming the competitor.

1. "Can your current provider let you change assessment wording without a change request?"
2. "What happens to your assessment data once someone is hired — does it inform their development plan?"
3. "How does your current platform handle the period between offer and start date?"
4. "How much of your assessment configuration can your team do themselves, versus what requires your vendor?"
5. "If you wanted to change the competency labels to match your own framework, how would that work today?"
