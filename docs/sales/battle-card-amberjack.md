# Battle Card: Amberjack

**Audience:** Dave (sales). Use this when Amberjack is the incumbent or named competitor.

---

## Positioning

Amberjack and Talent Edge sell the same thing: a managed graduate recruitment service. Amberjack has been doing it for longer. We do it on a modern platform that covers more of the lifecycle. The question for every prospect isn't "managed service vs tool" — it's "which managed service partner has the right platform underneath."

---

## The core difference in one line

> "Amberjack runs your program on a platform designed fifteen years ago. We run your program on a platform designed for how early careers works today — and we cover the three stages Amberjack doesn't: Keep Warm, Onboard, and Develop."

---

## When we win

- **The platform experience matters.** Program Managers who actually use Amberjack's platform day-to-day know it's dated. Teams that care about modern UX, fast iteration, and mobile-ready candidate experience are an easy win.
- **Assessment config needs to move fast.** Changing assessment wording, swapping competency labels, adjusting a report format in Amberjack requires a change request and a bill. Inside a Talent Edge engagement, clients change these themselves — same managed service, without the vendor bottleneck.
- **Companies caring about equity and DEI.** "Assess for potential, not privilege" is a more honest and more defensible story than Amberjack's model. A potential-based assessment levels the playing field. This resonates with HR Directors and DEI leads in particular.
- **Keep Warm gap is real.** Candidate dropout between offer acceptance and start date is a costly and unsolved problem. Amberjack doesn't cover this — clients either leave it unmanaged or bolt on Grad-Engage separately. Talent Edge runs Keep Warm as a connected lifecycle phase.
- **Want assessment data to inform development.** Amberjack stops at selection. The potential data gathered during screening never makes it into a development plan. Talent Edge carries the data through to Track Talent — it doesn't disappear after hire.
- **Amberjack contract up for renewal.** Renewal is the trigger event. Mid-contract deals are rare — target accounts approaching end of term.

## When we lose

- **Brand risk aversion in procurement.** Amberjack has strong recognition in Australian graduate recruitment. For procurement-led decisions where established brand outweighs product fit, we're the harder sell. Often solvable by bringing the HR Director or Program Manager back into the room.
- **Mid-contract, no trigger event.** Without a renewal window or a live pain point, there's no lever. Qualify out early and stay warm — note the renewal date and follow up.
- **They don't want a new managed service partner.** If the client is actively unhappy with all outsourced providers and wants to bring everything in-house, we're not the answer either. This is rare — 90% of the market doesn't have the in-house IP to DIY this well.

---

## Landmines

Questions Dave plants early to make Amberjack's limitations visible. Ask these in discovery — they surface pain without naming the competitor.

1. "How much of your assessment configuration can your team do themselves, versus what requires your vendor?" — Surfaces the bespoke-change cost.
2. "What happens to your assessment data once someone is hired — does it inform their development plan?" — Almost always "no." Opens the lifecycle gap.
3. "How does your current partner handle the period between offer and start date?" — Surfaces the Keep Warm gap. Expect "we don't really" or "we use Grad-Engage separately."
4. "When was the last time you saw your current platform's UI redesigned?" — Surfaces the modernisation gap. Amberjack's platform looks its age.
5. "If you wanted to change the competency labels to match your own framework mid-year, how would that work today?" — Forces the cost-of-change conversation.
