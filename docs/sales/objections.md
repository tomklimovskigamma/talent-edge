# Objection Handling

When prospects push back, use this. The goal isn't to "win" the objection — it's to understand the concern behind it and address that.

---

## 1. "We already use Amberjack"

**What's really going on:** Switching risk. They've invested in a relationship, a process, and possibly a multi-year contract. They're not sure what they'd be giving up.

**You say:** "Amberjack's been doing managed graduate recruitment for a long time — they know the space. Where we're different is two things. First, the platform you actually use day-to-day. Theirs was designed fifteen years ago — ours is modern, mobile-ready, and has AI-assisted screening built in. Second, lifecycle coverage. Amberjack stops at Select. We cover Keep Warm, Onboard, and Develop too — so the assessment data we build about your candidates actually flows through into how you develop them once they're hired. That's a gap no other managed service fills."

## 2. "We just renewed our ATS"

**What's really going on:** They think you're an ATS replacement and they've already spent their budget and political capital there.

**You say:** "We're not replacing your ATS — we integrate with it. If you're on PageUp or Workday, we sit on top. We're the assessment, selection intelligence, and development layer that your ATS doesn't do — and we bring a managed service team who runs your program end-to-end. Think of it like this: your ATS tracks where candidates are. We help you decide which ones to move forward, run the Assessment Centres and interviews, and then carry that data into how you develop them after they're hired."

---

## 3. "Psychometrics don't predict performance"

**What's really going on:** They've seen bad psychometrics. They've watched assessment processes that clearly favoured certain candidates and produced mediocre hires. The scepticism is fair.

**You say:** "You're right that traditional behavioural assessments often don't — they tend to reward candidates who've had the most opportunities to practice, not the ones with the most potential. That's exactly why we don't do that. We assess five dimensions of potential: adaptability, cognitive agility, emotional intelligence, collaboration, and drive. These predict how someone will learn and grow, not how polished they are today. For graduates with limited work history, that's the right question."

## 4. "This looks early-stage / it's just a demo"

**What's really going on:** Risk aversion. They don't want to bet their program on a startup that might not be around next intake. Totally reasonable.

**You say:** "You're right that we're early. What you're seeing is a working product, not a slide deck — but we're not pretending to be a 10-year-old enterprise platform. What we can show you is: the product works, the assessment model is sound, and we're building fast. We're also a managed service — our team runs your program, so you're not betting on the platform alone. The question for you is whether the vision — full lifecycle, equity-centred, with a services team behind it — is worth being an early partner. Early partners shape the product."

---

## 5. "We don't have budget for another tool"

**What's really going on:** Cost, but usually it's really about how costs are categorised. They're comparing a new line item against what they're already paying — without accounting for what they're already paying in time and workarounds.

**You say:** "We're not another tool — we're a managed service partner. If you're currently with Amberjack or a similar provider, this is a like-for-like replacement conversation, not an add-on. If you're managing this in-house, think about what you're currently spending across manual screening, spreadsheets, separate assessment vendors, and bespoke change fees. We consolidate that into a single engagement. The question isn't whether there's a cost — it's whether this cost replaces three others."

## 6. "Our grad program is too small"

**What's really going on:** They feel self-conscious about scale, or they think enterprise tooling won't work for them. Sometimes they're right — under 10 hires a year and the economics genuinely don't work for a managed service delivery model.

**You say:** If it's genuinely under 10, park it — come back when the program grows. For managed service delivery, under-10 truly is too small for us to deliver the service profitably, and we'd rather be honest about that. If it's 10–30: "That's actually where the pain is sharpest. You don't have a dedicated team of five running this — it's probably one person managing the whole thing across multiple systems. That's exactly who benefits most from having a managed service with everything in one place."

## 8. "Can we customise the assessment to our competency framework?"

**What's really going on:** This is a BUYING SIGNAL. They're not pushing back — they're asking if the product can work for them. They have an internal framework (often tied to performance reviews or L&D) and want to know if the assessment will speak their language.

**You say:** "Yes — and your team controls it directly inside our engagement. Open the Assessment Config, change competency labels, question language, scoring weights. No change request, no bespoke engagement fee. Most managed service providers bill every change separately — we don't." Then show them Assessment Config in the demo.

---

## 9. "Do you run Assessment Centres?"

**What's really going on:** They want full-service including AC delivery — they're checking whether we're a platform-only vendor or a genuine managed service.

**You say:** "Yes — our services team designs and runs Assessment Centres face-to-face. That's where human judgment matters most. What our platform does is capture the scores from AC assessors back into the candidate profile — so your psychometric data, AC ratings, and interview feedback all live in one place. You end up with a complete picture for the offer decision, not three separate spreadsheets."

---

## 10. "Do you offer video interviews?"

**What's really going on:** They're verifying that the platform scope matches what's been promised — they've been burned before by vendors who oversell capabilities.

**You say:** "Our platform captures video interview recordings and our services team facilitates the interviews. Video recording capability in the platform is shipping in our next release — for today's engagements we integrate with Modern Hire or HireVue if you use them, and our people handle the review."

## 11. "We want a fully managed service — we don't have time to use a tool"

**What's really going on:** They've had bad experiences with platform vendors who hand them software and leave. They want someone to run the program, not teach them to use software.

**You say:** "That's exactly what we are. We run your program — application through offer to first-year review. Your platform access is for visibility and control, not for you to do the work. You get weekly reports, pipeline status, candidate profiles — our team handles the execution."
